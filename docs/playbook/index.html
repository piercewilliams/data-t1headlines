Rules of thumb

  • Possessive + named entity on crime and business is associated with the highest consistent lift. Anchor to a specific person or company: "Target's layoffs," "Smith's arrest."
  • Number leads: use specific figures in the 11–20 range. Round numbers score 13 percentile points below specific numbers (p=0.04 * after BH-FDR).
  • Avoid question format for organic reach — underperforms 0.65× baseline (padj<0.001 ***). Reserve for Featured targeting if "What to know" is unavailable.
  • Don't truncate headlines to fit a format preference — median performing length is 80 chars.
  • Business and lifestyle have the widest outcome spread (IQR/median: 1.55 and 1.56) — headline choice matters most here. Prioritize variant production on these topics first.

  • Serial/escalating stories with a named anchor are the highest-CTR content type — structure updates as installments with possessive framing.

  • Longer headlines may proxy for longer, higher-stakes stories — confounders exist. Don't pad headlines to hit a character count; use the length the story requires.
  • Business and lifestyle show the widest performance spread — length optimization is most likely to matter there.
