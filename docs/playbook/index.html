Apple News rules of thumb
  • Possessive + named entity on crime and business drives the highest consistent lift. Anchor to a specific person or company: "Target's layoffs," "Smith's arrest."
  • Number leads are trending up (Finding 8) — use specific numbers in the 11–20 range; avoid round numbers (round leads underperform specific leads).
  • Avoid question format for organic performance — underperforms 0.65× baseline. Reserve for Featured targeting only if "What to know" is unavailable.
  • Longer headlines outperform shorter ones — median length is 80 chars; don't truncate to fit a format preference.
  • Business and lifestyle have the widest outcome variance — headline choice matters most here. Concentrate variant production on these topics first.

SmartNews rules of thumb
  • Local and U.S. National channels are severely underused at 1.85× and 1.81× ROI. Frame content with geographic specificity — "Sacramento," not "California," not "the region."
  • Reduce Entertainment volume: 36% of articles, lowest ROI. Reframe entertainment content toward lifestyle or local angles where possible.
  • Sports underperforms SmartNews (0.77× platform median) — don't rely on sports content for SmartNews reach; the same story with a local/civic frame does better.
  • Median headline length on SmartNews is 72 chars — see the length analysis in Finding 6 for platform-specific guidance.

Notification rules of thumb
  • Lead with "EXCLUSIVE:" on genuine scoops — 2.49× CTR lift. The word must be earned; overuse erodes the signal.
  • Named person + possessive: "Smith's connection to…" outperforms "Smith connected to…" (1.86× lift).
  • Write longer notifications: ≤80 chars gets 39% fewer clicks. Give readers context before asking them to tap.
  • Avoid question format: hurts notification CTR (0.64×), consistent with the article finding.
  • Serial/escalating stories with a celebrity anchor are the highest-CTR content type — structure updates as installments with named-person possessive framing.
