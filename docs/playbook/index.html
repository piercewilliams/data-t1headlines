High confidence Apple News · Featured Targeting

“What to know” is associated with 1.8× Featured placement. But for non-Featured articles, organic view performance trends lower (padj=0.10, not significant at α=0.05, n=16).

→ Reserve “What to know” for intentional Featured campaigns. Don’t apply it broadly for organic reach.

High confidence Apple News · Section Tagging

Articles published without a section tag (“Main only”) land in the bottom 20% at 47%—2.4× the baseline rate. Their median view percentile rank is 0.22, versus 0.51 for section-tagged articles (Mann-Whitney p=9.9e-36, n=318 untagged). Featured rate: 2.5% vs. 7.7% for tagged articles.

→ Verify every article has a non-Main section assigned before publishing to Apple News. Missing section metadata removes the article from section feeds and makes featuring nearly impossible. This is the most avoidable failure mode in the dataset.

High confidence Apple News · Local vs. National Content

National wire content (“Nation & World”) underperforms local-tagged sections by 26% percentile points (median rank 0.33 vs. 0.59; p=5.3e-19). 35% of Nation & World articles land in the bottom quintile. Top-performing sections are all locally specific: Weather (41% featured, rank 0.89), city-branded local sections, and franchise sports teams with national audiences.

→ Don’t use Apple News as the primary distribution channel for national wire stories. Apple News users follow local outlets for local content; they get national news from national brands. Route national wire through MSN or SmartNews instead, where platform topology differs.

Details ↓
High confidence Platform Headline Pairs · Same Story, Different Headline

The same story needs two different headlines. Apple News editors favor structured formulas (“Here’s”, “What to know”, questions) for featuring. SmartNews algorithmically penalizes those same formulas—question format drops to 0.42 pct_rank (p=3.4e–6), “What to know” to 0.37 (p=3.0e–6). “Here’s” is the only formula directionally above baseline on both platforms.

→ Write the Apple News headline first, then rewrite for SmartNews. See the topic-by-topic pairing guide below.

High confidence Push Notifications · Two Content Ecosystems + CTR Decline

News brand notification CTR has declined 29% over 9 months (1.77% in Jun 2025 → 1.25% in Mar 2026). Attribution language (1.33×) is the only consistent headline signal still lifting CTR across this declining baseline. News brands (1036 notifications, 1.41% median) and celebrity/entertainment (747, 4.01%) remain 2.8× apart in baseline CTR with non-overlapping signals.

→ Build separate notification playbooks for news vs. celebrity content. Use attribution language for news brands. Monitor the CTR decline monthly—the channel is maturing and benchmarks need updating.

Rules of thumb

  • "What to know" is a Featured targeting tool, not a views driver—Featured rate is 1.8×, but organic views for non-Featured "What to know" articles trend lower (padj=0.10, not significant at α=0.05, n=16).
  • Featured articles drive 2.9× views vs. non-Featured—treat the designation as a channel, not a side effect. It is worth targeting intentionally.
  • Don't apply "What to know" broadly—the view penalty for non-Featured articles makes it a poor default formula outside an explicit Featured campaign.

Rules of thumb

  • Lead with "EXCLUSIVE:" on genuine scoops—— CTR lift. The word must be earned; overuse erodes the signal.
  • Named person + possessive: "Smith's connection to…" outperforms "Smith connected to…" (1.17× lift).
  • Write longer notifications: ≤80 chars delivers 26% fewer clicks. Give readers context before asking them to tap.
  • Avoid question format: hurts CTR (0.87×), consistent with the Apple News finding.
  • Serial/escalating stories with a named anchor are the highest-CTR content type—structure updates as installments with possessive framing.

CSA Persona Configuration · Ready-to-Use Instructions

Copy these instructions directly into CSA persona configuration. Each rule is drawn from a confirmed or directional finding. Confidence tier shown in brackets.

Apple News Persona

  • [HIGH] Featured targeting formula: Use "What to know" or "Here's" format when the editorial goal is Featured placement. These are associated with 1.8× and higher featuring rates respectively.
  • [HIGH] Organic reach formula: Avoid "What to know" for non-Featured articles—organic view performance trends lower. Use possessive named entity or number lead for organic reach.
  • [HIGH] Question format: Apple editors over-select questions for featuring, but the algorithm penalizes them organically. Use questions only for intentional Featured targeting, not for general organic distribution.
  • [HIGH] Character length: Target 90–120 characters. Below 70 chars and above 130 chars both underperform the median.
  • [HIGH] Crime/Business formula: "Here's" format associated with 16% featuring rate for crime (n=89) and 14% for business (n=72). Use it when targeting Featured in these topics.
  • [HIGH] Sports: Formula choice does not affect featuring odds for sports content (0% across all formulas, n=22–52). Optimize for organic only; do not target Featured for sports.
  • [HIGH] Section tagging: Every article must have a non-Main section tag. Untagged articles land in the bottom 20% at 47%—2.4× the baseline rate.
  • [MOD] Number lead trend: Number leads are the only formula trending upward over time (Q1 2025 → Q1 2026). Lean into them; deprioritize question format which is trending down.
  • [MOD] Trendhunter content: Mind-Body, Everyday Living, and Experience content currently earns 0% featuring. Focus persona configuration on organic Apple News reach, not featuring optimization.

SmartNews Persona

  • [HIGH] Avoid question format: Question headlines drop to 0.42 pct_rank—0.08 below baseline (p=3.4e-6, n=918). This is the strongest single avoidance rule in the SmartNews data.
  • [HIGH] Avoid "What to know": Drops to 0.37 pct_rank—the worst-performing formula (p=3.0e-6, n=213). Never use for SmartNews.
  • [DIR] "Here's" is the safest cross-platform formula: Directionally above SmartNews baseline (p=0.038, does not survive Bonferroni at k=5). Use when writing one headline for both platforms.
  • [HIGH] Number leads trending upward: Only formula with positive trajectory across 2025–2026. Prioritize for SmartNews when topic allows.
  • [HIGH] Character length: Target 70–90 characters for SmartNews. 80–99 char optimal bin confirmed; 100-char ceiling has no statistical basis.
  • [HIGH] Direct declarative baseline: Unformatted subject-verb-object headlines are the SmartNews default—never penalized. Use as the fallback when no formula signal applies.
  • [MOD] Nature/wildlife/science: Discovery framing ("Scientists found", "Never-before-seen") drives highest-ceiling performance in General/Discovery content. Do not apply question or WTK format to these stories for SmartNews.

HIGH = p<0.05 confirmed finding. MOD = multiple-comparisons corrected or n-limited directional. DIR = directional (p<0.10). All findings are observational—use as configuration starting points; validate against post-publication performance data.
