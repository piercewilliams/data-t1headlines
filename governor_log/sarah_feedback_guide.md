# How to Give Feedback on the T1 Headlines Analysis
*For Sarah Price — reference when reviewing findings tiles*

---

## The short version

When you look at any tile on the site, just tell Pierce:

1. **Useful / Partial / Skip** — one word per tile
2. If "Partial" — which part matters and which doesn't
3. If anything would be more useful with a tweak — say what you'd need

That's it. No pressure to be thorough. Even a quick "tiles 3 and 7 aren't relevant right now" is genuinely valuable.

---

## Why your feedback improves the analysis over time

The system keeps a calibration record of what lands vs. what doesn't. When you flag something as not useful, it gets deprioritized in future runs — which means the next report has a higher signal-to-noise ratio and takes less of your time to review. The more you give, the sharper it gets.

---

## The three signals that matter most

### 1. Actionability
*"Could my team do something differently based on this?"*

- **Can act on it now** → most valuable. Note what the action would be.
- **Interesting but can't act yet** → still useful; we'll note what's blocking it (missing data, platform access, etc.)
- **Interesting but not our call** → helps us stop surfacing this type

### 2. Specificity
*"Is this specific enough to put in a style guide?"*

- "Avoid question headlines on Apple News" → specific enough
- "Longer headlines tend to do better" → not specific enough — flag it and we'll find the number

### 3. Context shift
*"Has something changed in what you're focused on?"*

This is the most important signal and the easiest to forget to mention. If your team's priorities shift — you're starting to focus on a new vertical, you got a new platform, you stopped running notifications for a while — **say so**. The analysis will be chasing the wrong questions otherwise.

---

## Suggested format when giving tile feedback

You don't need to use this exactly — freeform is fine. But if you want a structure:

```
Tile [number]: [title]
Rating: useful / partial / skip
Comment: [one sentence — what you'd do with it, or why it's not useful right now]
```

**Examples:**

```
Tile 4: Notification CTR by Formula
Rating: useful
Comment: Directly actionable — I'm sending this to whoever writes our push copy.

Tile 6: SmartNews Topic ROI
Rating: partial
Comment: The category rankings are interesting but I don't know what to do with them.
The formula-specific stuff within it is more useful.

Tile 9: Author Playbooks
Rating: skip
Comment: Not using this right now — our team structure makes per-author guidance impractical.

Tile 3: SmartNews Formula Performance
Rating: useful
Comment: The "avoid What to Know" finding is exactly what we need — counterintuitive and specific.
```

---

## Other things worth mentioning anytime

- **A question you're sitting on** — "I've been wondering whether X matters" is a direct input to what gets analyzed next
- **Something you saw elsewhere** — a platform update, a competitor's approach, a claim someone made in a meeting that you'd want validated or refuted
- **Something we found that turned out to be wrong** — if a finding didn't hold up when your team tried to apply it, that's critical feedback

---

*No need to be formal. Slack, email, or in our regular meeting all work.*
